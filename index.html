Charles Paris Music

Over Time Grind Productions est. 2011

Charles Anthony Paris (born April 30, 1980), professionally known as Brother OTG, is a Black American record producer, software developer, and author. He is the founder of CHARLES PARIS MUSIC. His production credits include collaborations with artists such as Beanie Sigel and Tone Trump, with his work featured on major networks including BET, MTV, and VH1. As a voting member of the Grammy Awards' Philadelphia and Chicago Chapters for over a decade, Paris has made significant contributions to the music industry. He also develops educational content and free web-based applications for music producers under the Charles Paris Music Official Site brand.

Early life and career

Charles A. Paris was born on April 30, 1980, in Urbana, Illinois. His early engagement with music included creating and distributing mixtapes and selling music for a local "Rap House." In the late 1990s, he relocated to Decatur, Georgia, where he worked at the 285 Flea Market and began installing car stereos. At the age of 19, he attended Atterbury Job Corps before formally commencing his music career under the moniker "I Double L."

While developing his music brand through his initial label, Midwest Ill Muzik Group (ASCAP), Paris also pursued a career as a Journeyman Maintenance Mechanic. In his thirties, he moved to Philadelphia, where he became recognized as a notable music producer. During this period, he established Over Time Grind Productions, LLC, which serves as the umbrella entity for his professional brand, now primarily known as Charles Paris Music. Concurrently, he maintained a successful career in automotive sales, selling over 2,000 vehicles in the tri-state area between 2015 and 2020.

Presently, Paris's focus is on music production education and serving as an advisor and consultant for brand building and music business strategy through his brand, SupplyTheWork, which operates under the Charles Paris Music umbrella.

Published works

Book

Paris is the author of "The Unforgettable Chronicle". This work presents a comprehensive synthesis of historical and theological knowledge, integrating various narratives from ancient Sumerian texts to contemporary theological doctrines. The book aims to provide a unified timeline of human history and cosmic events.

Web applications

As a software developer, Paris has created over 15 free web-based applications and utilities for music producers and creative professionals under the Charles Paris Producer Tools suite. These tools are designed for accessibility and functionality, requiring no registration or fees. The suite includes:

  • Audio Tools: A collection of utilities for audio manipulation, such as an Audio Trimmer, Normalizer, Reverser, and Speed Changer.
  • Image Tools: A set of tools for visual content creation, including an Album Art Cropper, Resizer, Watermarker, and Color Filters.
  • Creative Tools: Interactive applications like O.T.G Quotes, CPU Countdown Generator, and musical idea generators.

Services

Brand Consulting: Paris provides strategic consulting services for artists and creative entrepreneurs, drawing upon his experience in the music and business sectors. This service is designed to assist clients in developing sustainable brands, effective marketing strategies, and navigating the complexities of the music industry. Advice is provided on various aspects, including social media presence and revenue diversification.

Producer Mentorship: This one-on-one mentorship program is tailored for aspiring and intermediate producers, offering personalized guidance to accelerate skill development. Sessions cover advanced production techniques, workflow optimization, sound design, and the business aspects of professional music production.
