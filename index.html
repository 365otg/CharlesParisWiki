Charles Paris Music

Over Time Grind Productions, LLC, Midwest Ill Muzik Group, & Brother O.T.G

Charles Anthony Paris (born April 30, 1980), known professionally as Brother OTG, is an American record producer, software developer, and author. He is the founder of CHARLES PARIS MUSIC and developed the Over Time Grind business philosophy. His work includes production for Beanie Sigel,[1] the theme song for the BET series "Tiny and Toya 2,"[2] and tracks that have accumulated over 6.3 million plays on TikTok. Paris was formerly a voting member of the Grammy Awards and develops web applications for music creators.

Career Journey

Born in Urbana, Illinois, Paris began his career from 2002 to 2009 under the name "I Double L," founding the Midwest Muzik Group.[3] After winning local music contests, he signed a contract that did not advance his career. Following advice from Chaz Williams of Black Hand Entertainment,[4] Paris secured a publishing deal in 2009 with MC Lyte's[5] DuBose Music Group. Between 2009 and 2011, he developed a business plan and created music in preparation for a move to Philadelphia.

In 2011, Paris relocated to South Philadelphia and established Over Time Grind Productions, LLC. He worked as an engineer at Jazz U Up Studio and handled promotions for Glenn Lewis's single "Can't Say Love" with Ruffhouse Records.[6] From 2012 to 2016, he was the Social Media Manager and A&R for Phillybassd Records, where he mentored students from local universities. During this time, he was mentored by songwriter Bruce Hawes,[7] known for writing The Spinners' song "Sadie,"[8] which was later sampled in 2Pac's track "Dear Mama."[9]

The "Over Time Grind" Philosophy

Paris defines his "Over Time Grind" (O.T.G.) philosophy as the application of a blue-collar work ethic to creative industries, based on his past experiences in manual labor jobs. This approach, which forms the basis of his **Supply The Work** movement, involves undertaking a wide range of tasks to create opportunities, including photography, transportation for artists, and recording. His various professional aliases are now being consolidated under the **Charles Paris Music** brand.

The Tone Trump Era & Brand Development

For approximately a decade, Paris collaborated with artist Tone Trump, serving as President of his label. In this role, Paris was responsible for public image management, marketing, and road campaign management. This collaboration included Tone Trump's casting in the film *Creed*.[10]

The Beanie Sigel Era & Podcasting Influence

From 2015 to 2020, Paris worked with recording artist Beanie Sigel, managing his transition into digital media. Paris's role involved sourcing production from producers such as The Streetrunners[11] and assisting with business matters, including the patent for State Property, which preceded a group reunion. Paris produced Sigel's interviews on the Taxstone podcast,[4] which have been credited by some media commentators with increasing the popularity of urban podcasting. Paris also worked with Sigel during the Bad Boy Reunion Tour.

Marketing Innovator & Tech Visionary

Paris has developed and executed digital and street marketing campaigns for brands, including the retail store City Blue.[12] His work in this area led to his participation in industry conferences such as Ad:tech,[5] where he spoke about using automation for social media engagement. The CEO of the marketing technology company Rocketfuel commented that Paris's combined background in maintenance, automation, and music would make him a "fierce competitor."

His work has also included A&R for artists Helen and Terry Bruner,[13] consulting for brands and media platforms, and branding for the "Follow The Dream" campaign with Revolt TV host DJ Damage.

Community Engagement & Activism

Paris has stated that his community activism is influenced by his upbringing in the Midwest, the history of the Black Panthers, and events such as the Rodney King beating.[14] He was involved in organizing "Hands Up Don't Shoot" events in Philadelphia in 2014 and participated in the 2020 protests on the Vine Street Expressway.[15] His community work includes organizing local clean-ups, engaging with politicians, and outreach with the Nation of Islam. From 2008 to 2010, he coached youth sports at the YMCA, and in 2017, he participated in a Kidney Walk in Baltimore with the artist Freeway.

He has also conducted interviews with public figures including Chairman Fred Hampton Jr., political analyst Dr. Wilmer Leon,[16] and Tariq Nasheed. The subjects of these interviews are related to themes in his book, which discusses history, spirituality, and social theories.

The Rebirth & The Future

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Paris began using the social audio application Clubhouse to host a discussion room called "OTG Safe Space," which focused on topics such as history and culture. Terminology used in the room, such as "Rich Rising," was later adopted by other media figures, including Joe Budden.[7] Following this period, Paris rebranded his YouTube channel and began developing the **Charles Paris Music Academy**. The stated purpose of the academy is to be a media center that teaches musicians business and technical skills for an independent career.

Published Works

Book

Paris is the author of "The Unforgettable Chronicle", which presents a timeline of historical and theological events.

Web Applications

As a developer, Paris created the Charles Paris Producer Tools, a suite of over 15 web applications for music creators. The tools are available at charlesparis.online.

Educational Content

The Future: Charles Paris Music Academy

Paris offers masterclasses and personalized coaching for producers and songwriters. This work has led to the development of the **Charles Paris Music Academy**, a planned media center with a stated mission of teaching musicians the skills for an independent career.
